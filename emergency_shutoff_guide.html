1
STAY CALM & LOCATE YOUR MAIN SHUT-OFF VALVE
Common Locations:
  • Street-side: Near your water meter (usually in a covered box)
  • Basement: Where the main water line enters your home
  • Crawl space: Along the foundation wall
  • Utility room: Near your water heater

Philadelphia homes: Often located in the basement near the front wall or in a utility closet.

2
TURN OFF THE WATER SUPPLY

For Gate Valves: Turn the wheel/handle CLOCKWISE (righty-tighty) until it stops

For Ball Valves: Turn the lever handle 90 degrees so it's PERPENDICULAR to the pipe

Use force if needed: Valves can be stiff - use both hands or pliers if necessary

Valve Types

3
DRAIN REMAINING WATER

Turn on faucets throughout your home (start with the highest floor) to drain remaining water from the pipes. This reduces pressure and prevents further leaking.

Don't forget: Flush toilets to empty their tanks
