What to Do After a Storm Damages Your Roof

Your Step-by-Step Recovery Checklist from Philly Home Pros

Immediate Safety Steps (First 24 Hours)

  • Ensure Personal Safety: Stay away from downed power lines and be cautious of unstable trees or structures.
  • Prevent Further Damage: If it's safe, place buckets under active leaks. Do NOT climb on a wet or damaged roof. Call a professional for emergency tarping.
  • Interior Check: Look for water stains on ceilings or walls. Do not enter rooms with sagging, water-logged ceilings.

Damage Documentation Checklist

Thorough documentation is critical for your insurance claim. Do this before any major cleanup or repairs begin.

Exterior Photos:

  • Take pictures of the entire roof from all sides (from the ground).
  • Photograph specific areas with missing or damaged shingles.
  • Get close-ups of any hail dents on gutters, vents, or siding.
  • Document any fallen trees or large limbs on or near the house.

Interior Photos:

  • Photograph any water stains on ceilings and walls.
  • Take pictures of water dripping or pooling inside.
  • Document damage to personal property (furniture, electronics).
  • Keep receipts for any temporary repairs (e.g., tarps).

Insurance & Contractor Checklist

  • Contact Your Insurance Company: Report the claim immediately and get your claim number.
  • Call a Trusted Local Roofer: Schedule a professional inspection. A reputable contractor can meet with your insurance adjuster.
  • Get Multiple Estimates: Compare detailed, written estimates from licensed and insured local contractors.
  • Review Contracts Carefully: Never sign a contract that is blank or that you don't fully understand.

Avoid Storm Chasers & Scams!

Be wary of contractors who show up uninvited, pressure you to sign immediately, offer to cover your deductible, or demand large cash payments upfront. Always work with a licensed, insured, and local company like Philly Home Pros.
